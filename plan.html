Spatial Memory · Study Plan

How we test the concept. Full protocol is in the Full Doc, §8. Tasks are draft and may change.

0Claims

The work makes four claims. C2, C3, and M are tested by the studies below; C1, the phenomenon, is grounded in the literature (Full Doc, §1).

ClaimWhat we assert
C1 · phenomenonEncounter-class information is real and common, and today it dies because sending costs more effort than the small observation is worth.
C2 · captureRemoving the addressing effort, by capturing in place, increases how much people externalize in flow.
C3 · structureStructure, the Memory Graph, not anchoring alone, makes the accumulated record usable.
M · mechanismThe pipeline correctly structures real in-flow speech.

1How we evaluate

This is a concept paper, so we test whether the concept's claims hold, not whether the prototype is usable. The filmed week-long scenario carries the whole loop over time, and each small study isolates one claim. The studies chain: real utterances from Study 1 are structured by the pipeline and seed the rooms that Study 2 participants walk into, and the same corpus is the technical benchmark. Every number traces back to words real participants spoke while working.

EvidenceWhat it shows
Scenario, filmedthe loop end to end over a week (a demonstration, not data)
Study 1, capturepeople externalize more, at lower effort (claim C2)
Study 2, structurethe Memory Graph beats raw anchoring under accumulation (claim C3)
Technical evalthe pipeline structures real speech (claim M)

2Study 1 · capture (claim C2)

In-place voice versus phone and channel, within-subject. Both conditions wear the headset, so the only variable is the capture channel. Participants do a hands-busy task with planted encounter-class observations. We measure the externalization rate (of what they actually noticed) and the effort (time-off-task, NASA-TLX). Capture is wizarded, so behavior is measured cleanly and the pipeline is tested separately.

3Study 2 · structure (claim C3)

SM-full versus anchored-only on the same pre-seeded rooms, within-subject. The only difference is the graph, so any difference measures what the structuring component is worth. Anchored-only is what world-anchored annotation does today, so it is a real baseline. Two headline traps:

TrapWhat it tests
State resolutiona place's warning may be still live or already resolved; does the arriver act on the current state? (supersession)
Cross-place routinga warning left at one station must surface at another; anchored-only cannot route it (project memory)

Triage time and overload are secondary measures.

4Technical evaluation (claim M)

On the Study 1 corpus with gold labels: correct object and project filing, audience assignment, and supersession detection, plus anchoring reliability on the headset. This is where building the graph from messy speech is measured.
